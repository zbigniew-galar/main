### Project Manager's characteristics
#### Project Manager's main activity
Project managers spend 90% of their time communicating. 

Project Manager obligation is knowing "who needs what information and when" and making sure they have it.
#### Project Manager's obligations
- They must use clear, unambiguous exchange of information.
- They must be certain what the customer expects from the project.
- They must know what the project team is (or often is not) doing (constantly monitor the situation).
- They must keep key people well informed.
- Project team must know what is expected of them.
- People must know what they have to do, when they have to do it. 
- People must know what budget and time constraints and quality specifications they are working toward.
#### Project Manager's Areas of Expertise
- Application Knowledge, standards and regulations
- Understanding the project environment
- Management knowledge and skills
- Interpersonal skills
#### Project Manager's interpersonal skills
- Effective communication leads to better influence over people.
- Leadership leads to better motivation of project team.
- Negotiation skills lead to better problem-solving.

**Influence:**
Project Manager must have influence because Project Management is about getting things done. 

**Leadership:**
Leadership is the ability to motivate and inspire individuals to work toward expected results. Inspire the project team to see the vision and value of the project. Inspire the project team to find a solution to overcome perceived obstacles to get the work done.

**Motivation:**
Motivation helps people work more efficiently and produce better results. Recognition and rewards are an important part of team motivations. Some people don't like to be recognized in front of a group; others thrive on it. Team building is simply getting a diverse group of people to work together in the most efficient and effective manner possible. 

**Negotiation:**
Project managers must negotiate with stakeholders (mainly customers) for the good of the project.

**Delaying results negotiations example:**
I don't want it presented to the business before my own satisfaction:
Because I want to have full flexibility for innovations and more quickly bring it to the high maturity level.

**Problem solving:**
Problem solving is the ability to understand the heart of a problem (root-cause analysis), look for a viable solution, and then make a decision to implement that solution. 
If a project manager treats only the symptoms of a problem rather than its cause, the symptoms will perpetuate and continue through the project life. Even worse, treating a symptom may result in a greater problem.
#### The communication management plan
The communication plan answers the question of how the communication needs of the stakeholders will be met:
- the types of information that will be communicated
- who will communicate them
- who will receive them
- the methods used to communicate
- the timing and frequency of communication
- the method for updating the plan as the project progresses (the escalation process and a glossary of common terms)
#### Meeting culture
1. A meeting is necessary only when a decision must be made.
2. A decision cannot be made if all the people who could potentially object are not present.
3. Likewise, a decision cannot be made if not everyone who can contribute information for the best possible decision is invited to the meeting.
4. Therefore, if people who can provide information don't show up, the decision will be worse, but it can still be made. If there is no quorum, the first decision should be whether to reschedule the meeting.
5. If a decision is needed, a bad decision is better than no decision at all. The worst general is not the one who makes bad decisions, but the one who avoids them.
### Negotiations Basics
#### Definition:
When you are negotiating with someone you are trying to find out what they want and you are honest about what you want and you are trying to formulate a joint vision and plot a course forward for both of you that you are both very happy about and you would abide by voluntarily and if you're very fortunate that's gone be a long term reciprocal relationship. 

You want to know if the other person is not interested in that kind of outcome so that you don't waste time because the agreement won't last. And if you recognize that the deal won't last that is not a failure because you were negotiating in good faith to find out if there was something you could jointly do together. 
The tricky part is that there is a better outcome no matter what. 
#### Background
Empathy is understanding of the other side and it is not thinking the same.
Before I start to disagree with you I'll describe what your position is and I will have to describe it until you agree that I got it right.
Understanding through empathy creates oxytocin release and bonds someone one way ("that's right" is the sign).
The feel of understanding gives a feeling of satisfaction so you want less.
#### Voice
1. Assertive voice - direct and honest
2. Analyst voice - decision tree
3. Accommodative - hope driven - smiling, optimistic, hopeful (I hope this works out)
#### Before we start negotiating
I want to make you feel that I'm treating you fairly and if it's not the case tell me and we are gone address it.
#### Main assumptions
**World is abundant so we can fulfill our aspirations without zero sum game:**
Let's find a point in the future that we are both happy and how do we work our way back from there. 

**Set boundaries without negativity:**
I'm sorry that just doesn't work for me (I'm not gone choose that).
#### Questions to ask before I make a decision
1. How will this affect my future?
2. What I'm in a risk of losing?
3. How is this affect my identity?
#### What to avoid in negotiations
You should avoid taking a long time and not getting the deal. Walk away early if you feel that you won't get what you want.
You will know it in advance by observing clusters of behavior - warning signs that it's gone go sideways.
#### Psychology of negotiations
**Describe people's feelings in a few words. Labeling (calling elephant in the room hanging a label on an emotion):**
Brain is 75% negativity (stating it release that and prepare others for impact):
1. You sound angry
2. This is probably make you angry
3. I know you're angry
#### Negotiations end game
**They always must finish with the list of next steps:**
How do we proceed?
May I share with you some idea about how to proceed?
