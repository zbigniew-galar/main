### Conflict Management
#### Team building effort pays off
##### Prioritizing the Collective Over the Individual Ego
At its core, project work is a profoundly collaborative endeavor, completely divorced from individual ego. It is not a theater for comparing capabilities or tallying personal contributions; rather, it harnesses the team's collective capacity to achieve outcomes far beyond the reach of any single person. While it is certainly possible to complete a project without deep team bonding, true collaboration elevates the final result, whereas isolated efforts consistently yield mediocre outcomes. Ultimately, effective project management hinges on fostering this cohesion. When individuals cultivate mutual understanding and willingly surpass baseline expectations, the entire group achieves superior performance. The fundamental choice is clear: one must decide between joining a supportive, unified team or settling for a disconnected group merely performing the bare minimum for a paycheck.
##### The Internal Prerequisites for Membership in Great Teams
True professional pride stems not just from individual milestones, but from meaningful contributions to a larger team. Recognizing this requires an internal shift; a collaborative attitude must be deliberately cultivated, practiced, and internalized. This mindset is an absolute prerequisite for membership in exceptional teams. Only by developing these internal traits will an individual be invited to participate in highly impactful endeavors and earn the privilege of choosing their collaborators. Ultimately, the sheer volume of personal effort matters far less than how effectively that effort serves the group. Over time, teams inherently recognize an individual's competence, integrity, and dedication. By demonstrating unwavering honesty, diligence, and the conscious restraint of personal ego and emotion, a team member engenders profound mutual trust and enduring support.
##### Uplifting the Vulnerable: The True Measure of Leadership
A project is akin to a shared expedition; the entire group must reach the destination together. If a team member falters or sustains an injury during the ascent, the rest of the team must rally to assist, even if it means carrying them forward. In this context, an individual's raw capability is secondary to their willingness to support less experienced or struggling teammates. True leadership materializes precisely when the most talented members dedicate their energy to uplifting others. While the slowest individual may inadvertently create a project bottleneck, shaming them is counterproductive and jeopardizes the entire endeavor. Instead, the most effective response is to proactively ask how to help, offering genuine, practical support for the collective good.
#### Proper project documentation
Every enterprise entails more than merely constructing novel products; it requires executing tasks correctly and continuously acquiring knowledge to improve future processes. Furthermore, upon the conclusion of a project, we inevitably glean valuable lessons and accumulate both positive and negative experiences.

It is imperative to establish a single, objective source of truth for all participants. We must forge a consensus regarding past events to facilitate a civilized and productive dialogue concerning the outcomes for each individual.

Recollections and their associated emotions are deeply tied to the perceived fairness of project management and the overall team dynamic. Interpersonal interactions proceed much more smoothly when team members avoid debating past events based solely on fallible memory. Conflict is minimized when facts are not disputed—remaining readily apparent and immune to subjective interpretation—due to proper, written documentation.

Consequently, the sole remaining challenge during these discussions is to achieve an equitable assessment of each individual's contribution and overall value to the project, grounded in objective metrics. By aggregating these contributions, we establish the relative importance of each team member from the project's perspective.

The more comprehensively we document activities as they occur, the more objective our historical record becomes. As a direct result, we will significantly reduce interpersonal conflict and minimize time wasted on clarifying past events and current project status.
#### The KPI Dilemma: Balancing Metrics with Genuine Contribution
Every project must establish a set of Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) that function as an internal economy of checks and balances at the project level.

If individuals' contributions are overlooked or appropriated by others, it inevitably leads to anger, conflict, or the complete demotivation of the affected party.

People desire to be evaluated fairly, if not more favorably than they may warrant; therefore, any KPI framework must primarily prevent scenarios in which a person is undervalued relative to their actual contributions.

Implementing any KPI inherently creates a dynamic wherein individuals prioritize optimizing their metrics over maximizing their overall value to the project. They implicitly understand that unmeasured efforts will be forgotten, leaving only the performance captured by the KPI.

#### Speed versus Velocity
Productivity is measured by the velocity of progress toward the final goal, whereas speed is merely a measure of how quickly new things are developed and shipped. If you are going in circles, speed is irrelevant. Only velocity matters.
#### Resolving the conflict with Circle Map technique
The underlying assumption in this technique is that we can't change what is our lenses through which we see the world. We are their captive.
Therefore we need to externalize our thoughts. Turn them into objects that we can look at. Objects that can be examined, reshaped or discarded.

Once the frame is on the paper it moves from being the lens to objects we can debate and validate based on data.

How to avoid arguing about information about the topic when we arguing about the frame of reference (perspective from which we judge the information about the topic)?
We need to clearly state what is the information and what is the frame of reference.
**Visual execution of circle map is forcing to articulate frame and information separately:**
- Write the topic in the center.
- Around the center, write the context, or everything you know about the topic. The words in the circle are information about the topic. This information builds the definition of the topic.
- Then, draw a rectangle (the frame) around the circle. On top, write your point of reference for assessing the definition.

##### Professional problem example
- **Topic:** The Project
- **Inner circle:** The features project must deliver, the tasks, the code we are writing, the to-do list, stakeholders, everything regarding the project.
- **Frame:** Question mark for each member of the project team.

**Task 1 for the project team:**
Every team member should write down his frame of reference for the things that we are building together. Single sentence only. 

**Those frames of reference can be like:**
- **Engineer:** Stabilize the platform and reduce technical debt.
- **Product Owner:** Ship the next big and important feature.
- **Project Manager:** Fit into the budget.

We could then discuss different perspectives on the information about the project. 

The discussion may be lengthy and unproductive. However, we can avoid wasting time discussing information itself. The information inside the circle is our single source of truth that we all agree on. This consensus ensures that the discussion moves forward without any obstacles regarding the information we use to make conclusions.

But before we get into the discussion we can even refine the different frames into root perspectives to be even more precise in our discussion. We can ask what is the priority that is underlying each perspective. This priority must be then refactored from the end customer perspective because he is the only important part of the project. We are delivering the project for the customer. If he won't be happy with the product the project will be a failure. 
- **Engineer:** Stabilize the platform and reduce technical debt translates into assumption that the customer values stability of the product.
- **Product Owner:** Ship the next big and important feature translates into assumption that the customer values particular feature of the product.
- **Project Manager:** Fit into the budget translates into assumption that the customer is cost conscious and doesn't value any product that is delivered over budget.
